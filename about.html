Types of Toys Action Figures and Dolls: Represent characters from movies, TV shows, or original designs, encouraging role-play.

Building Blocks: Such as LEGO or wooden blocks, promote creativity, spatial awareness, and fine motor skills.

Educational Toys: Designed to teach specific skills, like math, language, or science. Examples include puzzles, counting games, and STEM kits.

Art Supplies: Items like crayons, paints, and modeling clay that encourage creativity and self-expression.

Outdoor Toys: Include sports equipment, bikes, and kites, promoting physical activity and outdoor play.

Board Games and Card Games: Encourage strategic thinking, teamwork, and social interaction.

Electronic Toys: These can range from simple battery-operated toys to advanced devices that incorporate technology and interactive features.
Benefits of Play Cognitive Development: Enhances problem-solving skills and critical thinking. Social Skills: Teaches sharing, cooperation, and empathy. Physical Development: Improves coordination, balance, and motor skills. Emotional Growth: Helps children express feelings and cope with challenges.
